## Why it matters
Any member of the GM group can append to the in-memory AI queue simply by replying
to one of the bot's messages; the queue has no size cap and each item drives an
outbound POST to the AI endpoint. A chatty or hostile group member can grow the
queue without bound (memory) and force sustained outbound requests (cost / rate),
and the input text is forwarded verbatim to the AI backend.

## Evidence
- `internal/bot/aichat.go:16-26` (`aiInput`): on any matching GM-group reply,
  `b.aiQueue.add(msg)` with no cap and no per-user limit.
- `internal/bot/aiqueue.go:19-24` (`add`): unbounded `append`.
- `internal/bot/background.go:153-190` (`aiResponderOnce`): pops and POSTs to the AI
  URL per item; the only pacing is `AI_INTERVAL` seconds between items, so a large
  backlog is processed slowly but the queue (memory) keeps growing while it does.
- The trigger requires GM-group membership (`aiInputFilter`, `aichat.go:30-38`), so
  exposure is scoped to that group's members.

## Proof / abuse case (defensive)
A GM-group member sends/var replies rapidly; the queue accumulates faster than
`AI_INTERVAL` drains it, growing memory and guaranteeing continuous AI-endpoint
traffic (billable / rate-limited). Each message body is sent off-host to the AI
service.

## Remediation (concrete)
- Bound the queue (drop or reject when full) and add a per-user enqueue rate limit
  in `aiInput`.
- Consider restricting who in the group can trigger the AI, and cap input length.
